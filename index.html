About Me

I am, Dimitris Gkoumas

I'm a Postdoctoral Researcher at Queen Mary University of London under the supervision of professor Maria Liakata. My research centres on the employment of Natural Language Processing (NLP) approaches to decipher dynamic language patterns for automatically extracting individuals' behaviour trajectories in mental health. Additionally, I hold a role as an affiliated AI consultant at the Huawei Ireland Research Center. In this role, I lead research efforts aimed at enhancing the understanding of programming language semantics and contribute to research that can be applied to Huawei product development. I hold a PhD from the Open University, UK under the supervision of Prof. Dawei Song. My doctoral research delves into multimodal representation learning for human language understanding, employing the mathematical formalism of Quantum Theory to introduce and deploy novel algorithms. My background lies in information technology (MSc, Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, Greece) and computer science (BSc, Technical University of Crete, Greece).

My Research Interests

Generative AI

Harnessing Large Language Models (LLMs) across the spectrum of temporality, reasoning, and causality for enhanced integration into real-world AI applications.

Evaluation

Formulating assessment frameworks for generative AI.

AI & Life Sciences

Exploring the potential of generative AI in Life Sciences to address the most formidable health challenges of our generation and contribute to the treatment of incurable diseases.

Multimodal Representation Learning

Research LLMs for multimodal representation learning to solve tasks spanning from agents for decision-making to medicine and robotics.

Quantum Information Theory

Researching the mathematical formalism of Quantum Theory for developing new algorithms.
